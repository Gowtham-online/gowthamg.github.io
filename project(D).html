Enterprise Analytics Dashboard — Case Study

Role: Solo UX Designer • Duration: 2.5 weeks • Tools: Figma, Miro, basic analytics

Project Overview

Problem: Product managers spent too long (60+ seconds) finding anomalies and exporting reports in the legacy tool. The product needed clearer visual hierarchy, faster drill-down workflows and export options.

Goals

  • Reduce time-to-insight (target: under 30 seconds for key anomalies)
  • Provide clear filters and drill-downs
  • Create export flows (CSV/PDF) with simple steps

Research

Conducted 5 quick guerilla interviews with product managers & analysts (peers). Key findings:

  • "I have to click too many filters to get the view I want." — PM
  • Charts lacked clear thresholds and highlights for anomalies.
  • Exporting took multiple steps and unclear naming.

Design Process

1. Information Architecture & Flows

Created a flow for: Landing → Identify anomaly → Drill-down → Export. Simplified the filter panel and added saved views.

2. Wireframes → High-Fidelity

Explored three dashboard layouts; chose a left filter + center charts + right details panel for quick inspection.

Prototype & Usability Testing

Interactive prototype created in Figma and tested with 5 users (remote). Key measurable result:

  • Average time to find first anomaly: 28s (down from 60s baseline)
  • Users found the export modal clearer and faster.

Design System

Created component tokens: colors, spacing, typography and chart legend patterns. Designed button/input variants and accessible focus states.

Outcome & Learnings

  • Reduced time-to-insight by ~53% (qualitative baseline + prototype tests)
  • Better developer handoff using tokens and component screenshots
  • Next steps: A/B test the saved view patterns and add anomaly alerts.
